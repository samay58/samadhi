# Product language

Use these terms consistently in product, code, tests, and evidence.

## Cadence

The runner's stable steps per minute. Cadence is sensed from movement and remains separate from any requested musical rhythm.

## Requested musical BPM

The rhythm Samadhi is trying to produce. In Auto it comes from stable cadence plus the runner's fine-tune. In Manual it comes directly from the runner.

## Native pulse

One plausible half-time, full-time, or double-time interpretation of an analyzed track tempo. A track can have more than one useful pulse interpretation, but only one should drive a given match.

## Track fit

How little a track must be stretched to reach the requested musical BPM inside the proven quality envelope. Good track fit is the coarse layer of adaptation.

## Tempo match

Music and cadence have the same frequency within the documented tolerance. This does not say that a beat and a footfall occur at the same instant.

## Beat lock

Musical beat position and footfall timing align. Samadhi must measure end-to-end phase and latency before using this claim.

## Felt synchronization

The runner can hear the tempo response and naturally settle steps onto the music. This is the product outcome. Tempo match is evidence toward it, not a substitute for it.

## Quality envelope

The playback-rate range that passed physical listening on the supported player and route. A successful rate write does not expand this envelope by itself.

## Auto follows

Samadhi follows reliable cadence, ignores sensor noise, chooses compatible music, and makes restrained corrections.

## Manual leads

The runner declares a musical BPM. Samadhi chooses compatible music and applies bounded correction without inventing measured cadence.
